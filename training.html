Pilot Career Pathway

Your roadmap from beginner to airline pilot with Sky-Bound Vision

Step 1

Private Pilot License (PPL)

Begin with the fundamentals of flight. Earn your private pilot certificate with 40+ hours of flight training, including solo flights and cross-country navigation.

  • Medical Certificate
  • Written Exam
  • Checkride Preparation
Step 2

Instrument Rating (IR)

Learn to fly in instrument meteorological conditions (IMC). This rating is essential for professional pilots and allows flight in low visibility conditions.

  • IFR Procedures
  • Approach Charts
  • Simulator Training
Step 3

Commercial Pilot License (CPL)

Transition to professional pilot training. Build flight time to 250 hours and learn advanced maneuvers, complex aircraft operations, and commercial regulations.

  • Complex Aircraft
  • Commercial Maneuvers
  • Multi-Engine Training
Step 4

Certified Flight Instructor (CFI)

Build flight hours while earning income as a flight instructor. This is the most common pathway to reaching the 1500 hours required for airline transport.

  • Teaching Methods
  • Lesson Development
  • Student Assessment
Step 5

Airline Transport Pilot (ATPL)

Reach the highest level of pilot certification. Prepare for airline operations with advanced simulator training and crew resource management.

  • Airline Operations
  • Advanced Simulators
  • CRM Training
